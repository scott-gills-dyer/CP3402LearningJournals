# Week 06  

## Learning Activities & Resources  
This week I didn’t do a lot of new learning activities, but I spent time reviewing my assignment feedback. I lost about 20 marks, mainly because my Team page was too general and there were some small inconsistencies across my sites.  

## Estimated Hours  
1 Hour  

## Content Insights  
I noticed that small inconsistencies across a site can impact the overall quality more than I expected. Things that seem minor while building can stand out when the whole project is viewed together.  

This week reinforced that good web development isn’t just about getting things working, but making sure everything feels consistent and well thought out. It also made me realise how important clarity is, clearly stating “contact for a quote” instead of implying pricing helps reduce confusion for users.  

## Career / Employability / Learning Insights  
This week was actually pretty relevant from an industry perspective. In a real job, attention to detail is huge. clients don’t just care that something works, but also how consistent and professional it looks.  

The feedback also showed how important clear communication is. Something as simple as clearly stating pricing or “contact for a quote” can make a big difference, as it sets expectations early and reduces unnecessary back-and-forth with clients.  

This ties into real-world development where small details and clear information can improve user experience and make systems more efficient for both users and the business.
