# Week 06  

## Learning Activities & Resources  
This week I didn’t do a lot of new learning activities, but I spent time reviewing my assignment feedback. I lost about 20 marks, mainly because my Team page was too general and there were some small inconsistencies across my sites.  

## Estimated Hours  
1 Hour  

## Content Insights  
Reviewing the feedback highlighted that consistency is not just a visual concern but part of overall system quality. Inconsistent wording, layout, or structure across pages can make a site feel less reliable, even if all individual components function correctly. This shows that a project needs to be considered as a whole system rather than a collection of separate pages.

It also reinforced the importance of explicit communication in design. Clearly stating information such as “contact for a quote” removes ambiguity and reduces the need for users to interpret intent. This is effectively a form of input/output clarity, where the system presents information in a way that avoids misinterpretation.

## Career / Employability / Learning Insights  
From a career perspective, this reflects the importance of quality assurance and attention to detail in technical roles. In infrastructure, networking, and systems-related work, small inconsistencies in configuration or documentation can lead to confusion, misconfiguration, or system issues. The same principle applies here, where minor inconsistencies reduce overall system reliability from a user perspective.

The feedback also highlights the role of clear communication in technical environments. Providing precise and unambiguous information reduces unnecessary interactions and improves efficiency, which is directly relevant to areas such as documentation, user instructions, and system design.

This week involved a different learning approach compared to previous weeks. Instead of relying on external resources such as tutorials or documentation, the focus was on analysing completed work and identifying weaknesses through feedback. This made certain issues, such as system-level inconsistency and unclear communication, more visible than they would be during initial development.
