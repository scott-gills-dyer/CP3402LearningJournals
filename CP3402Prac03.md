## Content Insights
This week helped me understand how WordPress is structured compared to Joomla, especially how pages are managed more directly instead of linking articles. I spent a lot of time navigating settings and using documentation, which improved how I approach learning unfamiliar systems rather than just guessing. WordPress felt more intuitive overall due to its simpler admin layout, but I still had to rely on external resources to understand how everything connects. Integrating Bootstrap also gave me insight into how front-end frameworks can be layered onto a CMS rather than built from scratch.   

## Career / Employability / Learning Insights
WordPress being so widely used makes it highly relevant in industry, especially in IT support and small to medium business environments. Many businesses don’t need full custom development, but instead need someone who can troubleshoot, customise layouts, or make small improvements efficiently. This week showed me that being able to quickly learn from documentation and apply solutions is just as important as technical knowledge. It also highlighted that understanding how to extend existing systems is a practical skill that can save time and resources in real-world projects.
